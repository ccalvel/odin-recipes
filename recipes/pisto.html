Pisto

Description:

Pisto is a traditional Spanish dish that resembles a ratatouille or vegetable stew. It is made by cooking a variety of vegetables, typically including tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, zucchini, and eggplant, along with garlic and olive oil. The vegetables are sautéed until they are soft and flavorful, often seasoned with salt, pepper, and sometimes paprika or other herbs and spices.

Pisto can be enjoyed as a side dish, served with bread, or as a topping for rice or eggs. It is known for its rich taste and versatility, making it a popular and comforting dish in Spanish cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • Tomatoes
  • Bell peppers (red and green)
  • Onions
  • Zucchini (courgette)
  • Eggplant (aubergine)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Steps:

  1. Prepare the vegetables by washing and chopping them into small, uniform pieces. Mince the garlic.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or frying pan over medium heat.
  3. Add the onions and garlic to the pan and sauté until they become soft and translucent.
  4. Stir in the chopped bell peppers and continue to cook until they begin to soften.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, zucchini, and eggplant to the pan. Stir well to combine.
  6. Season the mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Optionally, add other herbs and spices according to your preference.
  7. Cover the pan and let the vegetables simmer over medium-low heat for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until all the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded together.
  8. If the mixture is too dry, add a splash of water or vegetable broth to achieve your desired consistency.
  9. Once cooked to your liking, remove from heat.
  10. Serve hot or at room temperature as a side dish, topping for bread or rice, or as a filling for omelets or sandwiches.

Enjoy your delicious homemade pisto!
